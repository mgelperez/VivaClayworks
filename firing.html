Firing

Firing is the process of heating clay in a kiln to harden it and make it durable. It transforms the soft, dried clay into a finished ceramic piece that can be glazed, used, or displayed.

Custom Firing

Already well versed in the techniques and have a wheel at home but need access to a kiln? Bring your kiln ready pieces to us. Firing costs are based on spaced used in the kiln when firing cone 04, 06 and 6.


We will measure your pieces and give you an estimate. If you need a different firing service we will charge by the kiln load. If you are interested call us or email for more information.


Please ensure that you know exactly what kind of clay you are bringing to us. Not sure? Bring a photo of the packaging with you.


Please clearly and legibly sign each piece.



Types of Firing



Bisque

(cone 04, electric)


Hardens clay and prepares it for glazing. It removes all moisture, making the piece strong but still porous enough to absorb glaze.

Glaze

(cone 6, electric)


Melts the applied glaze into a glassy surface. It seals the piece and brings color, texture, and durability.

Lustre

(cone 018, electric)


A low-temperature firing that adds metallic or iridescent finishes. Often used as a final decorative layer on top of a glazed piece.

Wood Firing

(cone 10, wood)


A traditional method where wood ash and flame naturally create unique surface effects. Each piece reflecting its position in the kiln and the movement of the fire.

Salt + Soda

(cone 10, gas)


Salt and soda are introduced into the kiln at high temperatures, forming a distinctive glaze and subtle orange-peel texture on the surface.

Raku

(cone 10, propane)


A fast, dramatic firing process where pieces are removed hot and cooled in combustible materials which then creates unpredictable colors and crackled surfaces.
